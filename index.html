The timeline of Bobby Fischer's life

  • 1943 - Born in Chicago, Illinois
  • 1949 - Learned how to play chess from a chess set which his sister bought when he was 6.
  • 1950 - Moved to New York City.
  • 1955 - Started a membership in his first chess club in Brooklyn.
  • 1956 - Became the youngest Junior US Chess Champion. It was this year when Fischer's famous 'Game of the Century' was played against IM Donald Byrne.
  • 1958 - Became the youngest US Champion at only age 15, defeating long time masters such as Reshevsky, Lombardy and Bisguier.
  • 1959 - Played in the Candidates tournament, becoming the youngest ever to participate in it.
  • 1963 - Took a break from chess due to accusations of Soviet collusion but improved his chess in the meanwhile to make a big comeback to the chess scene.
  • 1970-1971 - Went on a 20 game winning streak embarassing grandmasters such as Mark Taimanov and Bent Larsen with a 6-0 score, qualifying himself for the World Championship match.
  • 1972 - Became World Chess Champion by defeating Boris Spassky with a score of 12.5-8.5.
  • 1975 - Lost his World Chess Champion title to Anatoly Karpov by default for not attending the match.
  • 1992 - Played an informal World Chess Championship match against Boris Spassky in sanctioned Serbia.
  • 2008 - Died in Reykjavik, Iceland on January 17th. His grave is situated in Reykjavik.
